Find Your FFit — Quiz Reference

A personality-led redesign of the 16-question quiz. Four Primary Styles × four Secondary Expressions = 16 outcomes. This document holds three views: the clean questions, the scoring logic, and the two combined.

1 · Questions 2 · Scoring 3 · Questions + Scoring
Section 1

The Questions

Exactly what the respondent sees — no scoring, no tags. Sixteen questions, four options each, framed around who you are rather than what you already own. Q14 is a flavour question: it feels identical to the rest but doesn't influence the result.

Questions 1–4 Mindset

How you think about design.
Q1Your ideal space feels most like you when it's…
  • ACalm, light, uncluttered
  • BCharacterful and personal
  • CRefined and timeless
  • DWarm and easy to live in
Q2You're instinctively drawn to pieces that…
  • AQuietly do their job
  • BDefine a room's character
  • CAre built to last a lifetime
  • DInvite you to kick back
Q3What you value most, deep down:
  • AClarity and calm
  • BPersonality and expression
  • CLongevity and proven quality
  • DComfort and ease
Q4A room finally feels right to you when it's…
  • ACalm and resolved
  • BLived-in and expressive
  • CPolished and enduring
  • DSoft and welcoming

Section 2

The Scoring

The engine on its own. Two independent axes are tallied across the quiz; the top of each combines into the result. Q14 scores nothing.

Final result = Primary Style (highest Style total)  –  Secondary Expression (highest Expression total).
e.g. Mid-Century – Artwork, Scandinavian – Lounge Chair, Classic – Table, Residential – Sofa.

Both axes are internally even (every category can win), so no style or expression is structurally favoured — the result depends entirely on the answers.

Tie-breaks. If two categories on an axis tie, the answer given to the highest-weighted question on that axis wins (Block 2 for Expression, Block 1 for Style). Failing that, the earliest-answered of the tied options takes it.
